## 2. The Hypothesis

**The ordered phase of the four-field substrate produces
general relativity as its effective field theory in the
long-wavelength, low-energy limit.**

More precisely: geodesics in the post-bang ordered phase,
described at the level of an observer who cannot resolve
the substrate structure, obey the Einstein field equations
to leading order.

If true, the factor of 2 between the eikonal prediction
(0.875 arcsec) and GR (1.750 arcsec) is not a failure of
the substrate. It is a consequence of working at the wrong
level of description. The substrate predicts 0.875 arcsec.
GR predicts 1.750 arcsec. Both are correct at their
respective levels.

## 3. Why This Is Worth Investigating

**Condensed matter precedents are real:**

Phonons in a crystal lattice propagate according to an
effective acoustic metric. The metric is not in the
microscopic Hamiltonian. It emerges at the level of
long-wavelength collective excitations.

Quasiparticles in a superfluid propagate according to
an effective Lorentzian metric. Again not in the
microscopic description — emergent at the collective level.

These are not analogies. They are established results
with explicit derivations showing how microscopic dynamics
produce effective metrics.

The NKP substrate is a candidate for the same structure
at a more fundamental level. The four-field system
produces an ordered phase with topological structure,
persistent defects, and long-range correlations. Whether
that ordered phase has GR as its effective description
is an open question with real motivation.

**Newton and Einstein pointed in this direction:**

Newton required absolute space as a background for
mechanics without being able to derive it or specify
its properties. The substrate provides a candidate
for what Newton was pointing at — an infinite medium
with local interactions whose ordered phase produces
the geometry Newton described.

Einstein’s equivalence principle — that locally all
physics looks the same regardless of gravitational
field — maps onto the local flatness of coherence
windows in the ordered phase. Not derived here.
But structurally consistent.

These are motivations, not proofs.

**The territory is genuinely unexplored:**

Standard cosmology defines the bang as the origin of
spacetime. Questions about pre-bang structure are
treated as meaningless within that framework — not
ruled out by evidence but by boundary condition.

The NKP framework treats the bang as a threshold event
in a pre-existing substrate. That’s a different starting
point. The theoretical territory between substrate
dynamics and effective GR has not been mapped because
the starting point hasn’t existed in this form before.

Bold hypotheses are sometimes right. Sometimes they
are wrong in interesting ways. This one is precise
enough to be tested.

## 4. What the Missing Derivation Requires

To establish or refute Path B requires:

**Step 1 — The four-field action with metric coupling.**

Write the action for the four-field system in curved
spacetime:

```
S = ∫ d⁴x √(-g) · L(α, σ, ν, J, g_μν)
```

This requires specifying how each field couples to
the metric. The minimal coupling prescription is a
starting point but may not be sufficient.

**Step 2 — Vary with respect to the metric.**

The stress-energy tensor from this action:

```
T_μν = −(2/√(-g)) · δS/δg^μν
```

This is the standard procedure. The result will show
whether the four-field system sources the metric
isotropically or anisotropically.

**Step 3 — Take the long-wavelength limit.**

Average over the substrate structure at scales much
larger than ξ_a. The effective description at those
scales should reduce to something. Whether that
something is Einstein equations is the question.

**Step 4 — Check the light deflection prediction.**

If the effective theory is GR, the light deflection
in the effective description is 1.750 arcsec. The
substrate-level prediction remains 0.875 arcsec.
Both are correct at their respective scales.

None of these steps have been completed. Step 1 alone
requires choices that are not uniquely determined by
the current framework.

## 5. What Would Falsify Path B

Path B is falsified if:

The long-wavelength limit of the four-field action
produces an effective theory that is demonstrably
not GR — for example, a scalar-tensor theory with
different post-Newtonian parameters, or a theory
with preferred frames.

Or if no consistent long-wavelength limit exists —
if the substrate structure doesn’t smooth out at
large scales in a way that produces any metric theory.

Both outcomes would be scientifically valuable.
A specific deviation from GR is a prediction.
No smooth limit is information about the substrate
structure.
