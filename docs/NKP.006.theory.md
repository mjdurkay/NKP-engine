## 3. What Geometry Mapping Requires

To connect α wells to gravitational analogs requires:

1. A candidate metric — something that maps α field
   values to distances or spacetime intervals
1. A geodesic equation — showing how excitations
   propagate through the α well geometry
1. A deflection prediction — the observable consequence
   of light passing through the well

Documents 007-013 address these in order. The first
geometric claim appears in NKP-THEORY-007.

## 4. Why This Boundary Matters

Documents 001-005 are substrate physics. Their claims
stand or fall independently of whether geometry mapping
succeeds.

The simulation results are real regardless of whether
the α well corresponds to a gravitational potential.
The screened Poisson equation is correct regardless of
whether light deflects as predicted.

The geometry mapping program in 007-013 builds on the
substrate results but does not validate them. They are
independently established.
